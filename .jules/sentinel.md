## 2025-03-03 - [Insecure Randomness for OTP and Backup Codes]
**Vulnerability:** Found `Math.random()` being used to generate OTPs in `BlockWalletDigi` and 2FA backup codes in `CredVerseIssuer 3`.
**Learning:** Developers sometimes reach for `Math.random()` for convenience, without realizing that its pseudo-randomness is predictable and not cryptographically secure, which makes OTPs and backup codes susceptible to prediction attacks.
**Prevention:** Always use Node.js' built-in `crypto` module (e.g., `crypto.randomInt()`, `crypto.randomBytes()`) for generating any security-critical tokens, codes, or secrets. Ensure `Math.random()` is only used for non-security related UI or testing logic.
